User Interface & User Experience

What are they and how do they differ?

Web Designer Depot - UI vs UX: What's the difference?

User interface is a fairly literal concept. It refers to the elements in a site that the user interacts with the website by. These can be buttons, sliders, links, input boxes, or anything else that responds to the users input.

User experience is a much broader topic however, that encompasses any sort of product that a company can produce for the use of their customers, whether it be the website, the products, or even the service provided by employees. The user experience is the impression made on the customer by their interaction with the company.

In terms of web design, this refers to both the styling and the function of the user interface. A user interface can be functional, but have mediocre user experience. It comes down to the ease of use, the clarity of the interface, the polish of the animations, and much more that make customer feel content and satisfied with their experience.

The key to such an experience is recognising the effect that shapes and graphics have on the user's emotions. Human perception is tied closely to emotion, so that even something as basic as a rectangle can carry emotional meaning by its aspect, its colour, its size, or the rounding of its corners. Every element, every colour, and every image forms a comprehensive whole that triggers a reflexive emotional response in the brain, and whether a user realizes it or not, they can be made to feel a particular way by subtle arrangements of elements.
